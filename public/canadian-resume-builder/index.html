What makes a Canadian resume different?

Canadian employers have specific expectations for resume format that differ from US, UK, and international norms. Here's what you need to know.

🚫

No Photo Required

Unlike many European and Asian countries, Canadian resumes do not include a photo. Adding one can actually hurt your application.

📅

No Age or Marital Status

Canadian human rights law prohibits discrimination based on age, sex, or marital status. Do not include these on your resume.

📋

Reverse-Chronological Format

Most Canadian employers prefer a clear chronological format with your most recent position listed first.

📏

One to Two Pages

Canadian resumes are typically 1–2 pages. Junior candidates should aim for one page; senior professionals may use two.

🏷️

Include a Professional Summary

A 2–3 sentence professional summary at the top is standard in Canada and helps recruiters quickly assess fit.

📊

Quantify Achievements

Canadian employers respond strongly to data-driven bullet points. Always quantify your impact (%, $, time saved).

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the standard Canadian resume format?

A standard Canadian resume includes: contact information (name, phone, email, city/province — no full address), a professional summary, work experience in reverse-chronological order, education, and skills. No photo, no age, no SIN number.

Should I include my full address on a Canadian resume?

No. In Canada it's standard to include only your city and province (e.g., Toronto, ON). A full street address is considered outdated and may raise privacy concerns.

Is a one-page resume standard in Canada?

For candidates with fewer than 10 years of experience, a one-page resume is ideal. Two pages are acceptable for senior professionals. Never go beyond two pages.

Do Canadian employers expect a cover letter?
