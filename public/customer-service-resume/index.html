What recruiters look for in a customer service resume

Customer service hiring managers want to see measurable results, specific tools, and evidence of empathy and communication skills.

📊

Quantify Your CSAT & KPIs

Include your customer satisfaction score (CSAT), NPS, first-call resolution rate, average handle time, and upselling metrics.

🛠️

List Your Tools

Employers want to see experience with Zendesk, Salesforce, Freshdesk, Intercom, or any CRM and ticketing system you've used.

🤝

Highlight Soft Skills with Evidence

Don't just list 'good communicator' — describe a situation where your communication resolved a difficult issue.

📞

Specify Your Channel Experience

Phone, email, live chat, social media support — specify which channels you've worked in and your volume per channel.

🌍

Languages Are a Major Advantage

Bilingual and multilingual customer service agents are in high demand. Always list your language skills and proficiency level.

🏆

Awards & Recognition

Agent of the month, top CSAT award, peer recognition — include any formal or informal recognition you've received.

Frequently Asked Questions

What skills should I put on a customer service resume?

Include both hard skills (CRM tools, ticketing systems, typing speed, languages) and soft skills demonstrated with examples (conflict resolution, empathy, patience, active listening).

How do I write a customer service resume with no experience?

Focus on transferable skills from school, volunteer work, or personal projects. Retail, food service, or any public-facing role counts as customer service experience.

What is a good CSAT score to put on a resume?

Any score above 90% is worth mentioning. If your score is 95%+, lead with it. If you don't have a formal CSAT score, mention your average rating or any customer compliments you've received.

Should I include a cover letter for a customer service job?

Yes. A short, warm cover letter that demonstrates your communication skills directly supports your customer service candidacy. Keep it to 3 paragraphs.
