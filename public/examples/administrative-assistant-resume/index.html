How to write an administrative assistant resume

Strong admin resumes prove reliability and software fluency, then back them with concrete examples of how you made an office run more smoothly.

🗓️

Highlight Organisation Wins

Calendar systems, scheduling, and process improvements with a result (time saved, errors removed) turn duties into achievements.

💻

List Office Software

Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, Outlook, and any scheduling or expense tools — these are frequent ATS keywords.

🤝

Show Communication Skills

Reception, vendor liaison, and executive support demonstrate professionalism and discretion.

📊

Quantify Where You Can

Calls handled per day, executives supported, records managed, and cost or time savings make your impact concrete.

🔒

Mention Confidentiality

Handling sensitive scheduling, finances, or HR information shows you can be trusted with discretion.

✅

Keep It Clean & ATS-Safe

An orderly, single-column layout mirrors the organisation employers expect — and parses well in ATS.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should an administrative assistant resume include?

A brief summary, office software skills, core competencies (scheduling, travel, expenses, communication), work experience with quantified efficiency wins, and education or relevant certifications.

How do I write an admin assistant resume with no experience?

Emphasise transferable skills from school, volunteering, or other jobs — organisation, communication, software, and customer service. Highlight any responsibility for scheduling, records, or coordination.

What's the difference between an administrative assistant and an executive assistant resume?

An executive assistant resume emphasises supporting senior leaders, confidentiality, complex calendar and travel management, and higher autonomy. An administrative assistant resume covers broader general office support.

How long should an administrative assistant resume be?

One page is standard and preferred for most administrative roles. Only extend to two pages with extensive, directly relevant experience.
