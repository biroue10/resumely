UK CV format — what British employers expect

The British CV has specific conventions that differ from American resumes and other international formats. Here's what UK hiring managers look for.

📄

Two Pages is Standard

Unlike in the US where one page is preferred, UK CVs are typically 2 pages for anyone with more than 3 years of experience.

👤

Personal Statement at the Top

A 3–4 sentence personal statement (sometimes called a professional profile) is standard at the top of a UK CV.

📸

No Photo Required

UK CVs do not typically include a photo. Including one can expose you to appearance-based discrimination and is generally discouraged.

🏠

Address is Optional

A full postal address used to be standard but is now optional. Including your town or city is sufficient.

🎓

A-Levels and GCSEs for Graduates

UK graduates typically include A-level subjects and grades, and sometimes GCSE results in English and Maths.

📋

Hobbies & Interests Section

A brief hobbies section is more common in UK CVs than in US resumes. Keep it to 2–3 lines and link interests to professional skills where possible.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the standard UK CV format?

Personal statement, work experience in reverse-chronological order, education (including A-levels and GCSEs for graduates), skills, and optionally hobbies/interests. No photo, no nationality, no NI number. Typically 2 pages.

Should I include a personal statement on a UK CV?

Yes. A 3–4 sentence personal statement at the top of your CV is standard practice in the UK. It should summarise your experience, key skills, and career objective.

What is the difference between a CV and a resume in the UK?

In the UK, 'CV' and 'resume' are used interchangeably, but 'CV' is the dominant term. UK CVs are typically 2 pages versus the US preference for 1 page, and include a personal statement and sometimes hobbies.

Should I include references on a UK CV?

Modern UK CVs include the phrase 'References available on request' at the bottom or simply omit the references section entirely. Do not include actual reference details unless specifically asked.
