How to write a teacher resume

Teaching resumes are screened for certification, grade levels, and subjects first — then for evidence that your students actually learned and grew.

🪪

Certification & Licensure First

State teaching licence, grade band (K–6, 7–12), and subject endorsements belong near your name — they're primary screening criteria.

📈

Show Student Outcomes

Proficiency gains, test-score growth, and graduation or pass rates are the strongest evidence of effective teaching.

🧩

Highlight Instructional Strategies

Differentiated instruction, project-based learning, SEL, and IEP support show range and adaptability.

💻

List EdTech Tools

Google Classroom, Canvas, Seesaw, and assessment platforms are increasingly expected — name the ones you use.

🤝

Include Collaboration & Leadership

Grade-level teams, curriculum committees, mentoring, and clubs demonstrate contribution beyond your own classroom.

✅

Keep It ATS-Safe

District hiring systems use ATS. Use clear headings and avoid graphics so your certifications and subjects parse correctly.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should a teacher resume include?

A summary, your teaching certification and grade levels/subjects, classroom experience with measurable student outcomes, instructional strategies, EdTech tools, education, and any leadership or committee work.

How do I write a teacher resume for a first teaching job?

Lead with your degree and certification, then highlight student teaching, practicums, and long-term subbing. Describe lessons you planned, strategies you used, and any measurable progress your students made.

Should I include test scores or student data on my teaching resume?

Yes, when you can frame it as your contribution — e.g. 'raised class reading proficiency by 18 points' — using aggregate, non-identifying figures rather than individual student data.

How long should a teacher resume be?

One page for new and early-career teachers; two pages is acceptable for experienced educators with extensive relevant teaching, leadership, and professional development.
