Help Desk Analyst resume tips

Hiring managers for Help Desk Analyst roles want to see measurable performance, a broad toolset, and clear communication skills.

📊

Ticket Volume & SLA Compliance

State your average daily/weekly ticket volume and your SLA compliance rate. These are the primary KPIs for help desk roles.

🛠️

ITSM Tool Proficiency

ServiceNow, Jira Service Management, Freshservice, Zendesk — hiring managers filter by specific tools, so list them all.

🔄

Incident & Change Management

Experience with the ITIL incident, change, and problem management processes is valued at mid and senior help desk levels.

📞

Multi-Channel Support

Phone, email, chat, self-service portal — specify the channels you've handled and your relative volume on each.

📝

Knowledge Base Contributions

Creating and maintaining KB articles directly reduces ticket volume. Quantify your contribution (articles written, views, ticket deflection).

🎓

ITIL Certification

ITIL 4 Foundation is the most relevant certification for help desk analysts. It demonstrates understanding of ITSM best practices.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between a Help Desk Analyst and an IT Support Technician?

A Help Desk Analyst typically works in a structured ITIL environment with formal SLAs, ticket management, and reporting responsibilities. An IT Support Technician often has a broader, more hands-on role including hardware repair and on-site support.

What ITIL skills should I list on a Help Desk resume?

Incident management, problem management, change management, and service request fulfilment are the core ITIL processes relevant to help desk roles.

How do I write a Help Desk resume objective?

Write a 2–3 sentence summary that includes your years of experience, your top tools (e.g., ServiceNow), your strongest metric (e.g., 98% SLA compliance), and the type of role you're seeking.

Is an ITIL certification worth getting for help desk roles?

Yes. ITIL 4 Foundation is quick to obtain (a few days of study), widely recognised, and expected by many enterprise employers. It demonstrates commitment to professional ITSM standards.
