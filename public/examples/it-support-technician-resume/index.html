How to write an IT Support Technician resume

An effective IT Support Technician resume leads with certifications, quantifies SLA metrics, and lists every tool and OS you've worked with.

🏅

Certifications in the Header

Put CompTIA A+, ITIL, or Microsoft certs directly after your name in the header so they're seen immediately.

📊

SLA & CSAT Metrics

First-call resolution %, average handle time, CSAT score, ticket volume — these numbers tell the story of your performance.

🛠️

Technical Skills Section

List operating systems, ticketing tools, remote desktop software, networking basics, and security tools.

💡

Problem-Solving Examples

Describe specific issues you resolved — the more concrete and unusual, the more memorable your resume.

📝

Documentation Contributions

If you wrote KB articles or SOPs, mention them. It shows initiative beyond ticket resolution.

🎓

Include Ongoing Learning

Self-study, online courses (Udemy, Coursera, Microsoft Learn), and lab work show ambition and continuous improvement.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should an IT Support Technician resume include?

Contact info, professional summary, technical skills, work experience with SLA metrics, education, and certifications. List every tool and OS you've used professionally.

How do I get an IT support job with no experience?

Obtain a CompTIA A+ certification, build a home lab, and apply for internships, apprenticeships, or junior help desk roles. Include any volunteer IT work and personal projects.

What is a CompTIA A+ certification and is it worth getting?

CompTIA A+ is the most widely recognised entry-level IT certification. It validates hardware, software, and troubleshooting knowledge and is required or preferred by many employers. It is worth getting for anyone starting in IT support.

How much do IT Support Technicians earn?

In the US, IT Support Technicians earn $40,000–$65,000 per year on average. Certifications, specialisations, and location significantly affect salary.
